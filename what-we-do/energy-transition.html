Energy Transition & Low Carbon Development

Accelerating Indonesia's shift to a sustainable, low-carbon energy future through evidence-based solutions and implementation support.

Why This Matters

Indonesia faces a critical juncture in its energy development. As the world's fourth-largest emitter, the country must balance economic growth with climate commitments.

With energy demand projected to grow significantly over the coming decades, Indonesia needs to rapidly scale energy efficiency measures, accelerate renewable energy deployment, and transform industrial processes—all while maintaining energy security and affordability.

The stakes are high: without decisive action, Indonesia risks being locked into high-carbon infrastructure for decades, jeopardizing both its climate goals and long-term economic competitiveness.

IREEM's Role

Four complementary approaches to drive systemic change in Indonesia's energy sector.

Research & Technical Studies

We conduct rigorous technical assessments, feasibility studies, and energy modelling exercises that provide the evidence base for policy decisions and investment planning.

  • • Energy baseline assessments
  • • Technology feasibility studies
  • • Energy modelling and scenarios
  • • Cost-benefit analysis

Policy Advisory & Regulatory Support

We work with government agencies to develop policies, regulations, and strategic plans that enable the energy transition at national and sub-national levels.

  • • Regulatory recommendations
  • • Strategic plan development
  • • Stakeholder coordination
